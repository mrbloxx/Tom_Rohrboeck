# Research Methodology

## Overview

This document outlines the systematic approach used in investigating Tom Rohrböck's alleged Russian intelligence operations and political influence networks. All research methods adhere to open-source intelligence (OSINT) standards and journalistic verification protocols.

---

## Research Framework

### 1. Intelligence Collection Methods

#### Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT)
**Primary Sources:**
- Investigative journalism reports
- Official parliamentary records
- Corporate registry databases
- Court documents and legal filings
- Academic research publications

**Digital Sources:**
- News media archives
- Social media platforms
- Video documentation
- Podcast transcripts
- Government publications

#### Human Intelligence (HUMINT) Integration
**Public Testimony:**
- Politician interviews and statements
- Journalist witness accounts
- Expert commentary and analysis
- Insider disclosures (where legally obtained)

**Network Analysis:**
- Relationship mapping from public records
- Corporate structure analysis
- Financial flow tracing
- Communication pattern identification

### 2. Source Verification Protocol

#### Multi-Source Corroboration
**Three-Source Minimum Rule:**
- Each major claim requires minimum 3 independent sources
- Sources must be reasonably independent
- Cross-verification across different source types
- Temporal consistency validation

**Source Reliability Assessment:**
- **Tier 1 (High):** Public broadcasting (ARD/ZDF), major newspapers (FAZ, SZ, DIE ZEIT)
- **Tier 2 (Medium):** Commercial news outlets, specialized investigative platforms
- **Tier 3 (Low):** Social media, blogs, anonymous sources (used only with corroboration)

#### Document Authentication
**Official Records:**
- Parliamentary inquiry verification
- Corporate registration confirmation
- Court filing validation
- Government publication authentication

**Media Verification:**
- Publication date confirmation
- Author credibility assessment
- Editorial standards review
- Correction policy examination
